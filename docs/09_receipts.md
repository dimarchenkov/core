# Receipts

## Purpose

Receipt records what was delivered by a Supplier.

It answers:

- from whom the goods arrived;
- when they arrived;
- which CatalogVariants arrived;
- in what quantity;
- at what purchase price.

Receipt does not create catalog entities and does not change stock by itself.

## Related responsibilities

- Supplier answers: who supplied the goods?
- Intake answers: what is this new catalog item?
- Receipt answers: what arrived in this delivery?
- StockMovement answers: what changed inventory?
- StockBalance answers: how much is currently available?

## Catalog items

ReceiptItem always references a CatalogVariant.

If the Variant does not exist:

1. Register Product and Variant through Intake.
2. Return to the Receipt.
3. Attach the newly created Variant.

Receipt may contain:

- existing Variants;
- new Variants created through Intake;
- a mixture of both.

Receipt itself must not duplicate Product or Variant creation logic.

## Lifecycle

Statuses:

- draft
- posted
- cancelled

Only draft behavior is implemented initially.

A draft Receipt:

- may be edited;
- may have items added, updated or removed;
- does not create StockMovement;
- does not affect stock balance.

Posting and cancellation are implemented together with StockMovement later.

## Receipt

Fields:

- number
- supplier_id
- receipt_date
- status
- source_document_number
- notes

Rules:

- number is generated by Core;
- number format is `REC-000001`;
- number is immutable;
- Supplier must be active and non-deleted;
- receipt_date is the business date of delivery;
- created_at is the technical creation timestamp;
- an empty draft is allowed;
- an empty Receipt cannot be posted in the future.

## ReceiptItem

Fields:

- receipt_id
- variant_id
- quantity
- purchase_price

Rules:

- Variant must be active and non-deleted;
- quantity is a positive integer;
- purchase_price is the unit purchase price;
- purchase_price uses Decimal and must not use float;
- purchase_price may be zero for samples or free goods;
- multiple lines may reference the same Variant;
- items may only be changed while Receipt is draft.

## Stock

Draft Receipt does not affect stock.

Future posting will create StockMovement records in one transaction.

A posted Receipt must not be freely edited or deleted.
Corrections will use explicit cancellation or reversal operations.

## Invariants

- Receipt number is immutable.
- Receipt always belongs to exactly one Supplier.
- ReceiptItem always belongs to exactly one Receipt.
- ReceiptItem always references exactly one CatalogVariant.
- Draft Receipt never changes inventory.
- Posted Receipt cannot be freely modified.
- Receipt does not create Product or Variant.
- Intake is responsible for catalog registration.
